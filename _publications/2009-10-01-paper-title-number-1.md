excerpt: "**Abstract**: The 21 cm signal offers a unique observational window into the evolution and large-scale struc-
ture of the early Universe. In response to the challenges associated with measuring the 21 cm
power spectrum, a variety of novel statistical methods have been developed in recent years, in-
cluding analysis pipelines designed to model one’s primary beam. Among the principal limitations
currently facing the field are the computational costs associated with primary beam model evalu-
ation, whereby accurate characterization of the beam requires precise interpolation methods, such
as those implemented in pyuvdata. In this work, we benchmark an alternative beam emulator,
MEDEA, against pyuvdata, with the specific objective of reducing model evaluation time along
the frequency-axis. We find that MEDEA, in its default configuration, does not offer a reduction in
evaluation time relative to pyuvdata. However, through targeted optimizations to the underlying
implementation, evaluation times can be reduced by factors ≥ 3 depending on the computational
environment, demonstrating that MEDEA represents an efficient alternative for primary beam mod-
eling."
